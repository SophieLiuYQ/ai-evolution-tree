## Benchmark step-change vs GPT-3.5

| Benchmark | GPT-3.5 | GPT-4 | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| MMLU (5-shot) | 70.0% | 86.4% | +16.4 |
| Uniform Bar Exam | 10th %ile | 90th %ile | 80 percentile |
| USMLE | ~50% | ~87% | +37 |
| HumanEval (Python) | 48.1% | 67.0% | +18.9 |
| GRE Verbal | 63rd %ile | 99th %ile | 36 percentile |
| AP Calculus BC | 43% | 51% | +8 |
| AMC 12 Math | 30th %ile | 84th %ile | 54 percentile |

These are the largest single-release benchmark jumps in the modern LLM era. No subsequent model has matched the magnitude.

## The opacity norm

GPT-4's non-disclosure of size/architecture established a new norm: frontier model providers publish capabilities, not specifications. Every subsequent OpenAI release (GPT-4 Turbo, GPT-4o, o1, GPT-5) has continued this pattern. Anthropic's Claude releases and Google's Gemini releases have largely followed. Only Meta (Llama) and a few Chinese labs (DeepSeek, Qwen) have continued the older "publish weights + specs" tradition.

The normative shift matters: it has made independent third-party evaluation, scaling-law estimation, and regulatory oversight structurally harder.
