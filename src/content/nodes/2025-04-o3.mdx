## What it is

OpenAI's o-series flagship released April 16, 2025 (o3 + smaller sibling o4-mini). The architectural / training contributions are not publicly detailed, but the externally-observed capabilities show two key advances over o1:

1. **Native tool use during reasoning** — the model can autonomously decide to run code, search, or call other tools mid-thought, with results fed back into the reasoning trace. This is "agentic reasoning" baked into the base model, not bolted on at the application layer.

2. **Visual reasoning** — o3 can reason over images as part of the thought process, including ARC-AGI-style abstract grid puzzles where it must induce rules from few examples.

The ARC-AGI gap (o3's 75.7% vs nearest peer ~25%) was the single most discussed result in AI of the first half of 2025. The benchmark's creator (François Chollet) revised his framing of what ARC-AGI measured in light of the result.

## Inference economics

o3 querying the high-compute "high effort" mode reportedly costs OpenAI $20-2000 per task (per the ARC-AGI benchmark write-up). The consumer-tier o3 in ChatGPT Pro uses much shorter reasoning traces (~$1-5 per query economic equivalent), with throughput limits.

This established a new pricing architecture: API tiers stratified by both model AND inference-compute budget per query, not just model alone.
