## What it is

A chat-tuned LLM productized as an API and consumer chat interface (claude.ai). Architecturally undisclosed in detail, but believed to be a decoder-only transformer in the 50-100B parameter range, post-trained with Anthropic's Constitutional AI recipe.

The notable spec: a context window of ~9,000 tokens at launch, expanded to 100,000 tokens with Claude 1.3 in May 2023 — at the time, ~25× larger than GPT-4's 8,000-token default and 12× larger than the GPT-4 32k variant.

Claude 1 launched on the same day as GPT-4 — a coincidence widely read as deliberate. The strategic goal was to establish that there was a meaningful "second" within the news cycle of the dominant launch.

## The 100k context window

Claude 1.3's 100k-token context (announced May 2023) was the single feature that drove enterprise adoption. Use cases: summarize entire books, analyze full SEC filings, ingest large codebases. GPT-4 didn't match it until GPT-4-Turbo in November 2023 (128k).

The technical underpinning was likely some combination of:
- ALiBi or similar attention-bias positional schemes
- Sparse attention patterns
- Memory-efficient attention implementations (FlashAttention, by then standard)

Anthropic did not publish architectural details, which is itself a tell about how the lab had shifted from "research lab" to "model lab" by mid-2023.
