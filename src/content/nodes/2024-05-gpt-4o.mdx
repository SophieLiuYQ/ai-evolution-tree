|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| GPT-4 Turbo | 2023-11 | — | ~85% | cascaded | native | 128k |
| Claude 3 Opus | 2024-03 | 86.8% | 84.9% | text-only | native | 200k |
| Gemini 1.5 Pro | 2024-02 | 83.7% | 71.9% | native | native | 1M |
| **GPT-4o** | **2024-05** | **88.7%** | **90.2%** | **native (~320ms)** | **native** | **128k** |
| GPT-4o mini | 2024-07 | 82.0% | 87.2% | native | native | 128k |
| Claude 3.5 Sonnet | 2024-06 | 88.7% | 92.0% | text-only | native | 200k |

GPT-4o was the frontier for ~3 weeks until Claude 3.5 Sonnet (2024-06-20) matched or exceeded it on most text and code benchmarks. The voice and free-tier distribution were the durable competitive moves; the benchmark lead was transient.

## The GPT-4o mini pricing reset

July 18, 2024: OpenAI launched GPT-4o mini at $0.15 / $0.60 per million input/output tokens — ~60% cheaper than Claude Haiku and cheaper than Llama 3 70B hosted at most inference platforms. Performance: MMLU 82.0%, HumanEval 87.2% — i.e., GPT-4 quality from 15 months earlier at 1/200th the price.

This was the single largest LLM price cut of 2024 and forced the entire tier to reprice within weeks (Claude Haiku → $0.25/$1.25, Gemini Flash → $0.075/$0.30). The floor of LLM inference pricing is now limited by inference compute cost, not by margin.
