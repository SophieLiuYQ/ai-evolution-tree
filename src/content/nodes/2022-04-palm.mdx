## What it is

A 540B-parameter decoder-only transformer trained on Google's Pathways infrastructure across 6144 TPU v4 chips — the largest single-model training run publicly disclosed at the time. Architecturally introduced multi-query attention (later universally adopted) and SwiGLU activations.

## How it compares to peers in 2022

| Model | Date | Org | Params | Train tokens | MMLU | Released? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| GPT-3 | 2020-05 | OpenAI | 175B | 300B | ~44% | API |
| Gopher | 2021-12 | DeepMind | 280B | 300B | 60.0% | no |
| Megatron-Turing | 2022-01 | MS+Nvidia | 530B | 270B | ~50% | no |
| Chinchilla | 2022-03 | DeepMind | 70B | 1.4T | 67.5% | no |
| **PaLM** | **2022-04** | **Google** | **540B** | **780B** | **69.3%** | **API only, late 2023** |
| GPT-4 | 2023-03 | OpenAI | undisclosed | undisclosed | 86.4% | API + product |

PaLM was state of the art for ~6 months. Then the world stopped caring about benchmarks because ChatGPT shipped to consumers.

## What PaLM contributed technically

- **Multi-query attention** — share K and V projections across attention heads. Reduces inference KV-cache memory by ~10× with minimal quality loss. Universally adopted (GQA in Llama 2, GPT-4, Claude).
- **SwiGLU** — gated linear unit activation. Mild quality lift; standard in modern LLMs.
- **Pathways infrastructure** — the cross-pod orchestration layer that made 6000-chip jobs tractable. Internal Google asset, never open-sourced.

The architectural lessons survive in Gemini and beyond; the PaLM brand was retired in late 2024.
