## Kling lineage

| Version | Date | Key capability |
|---|---|---|
| Kling 1.0 | 2024-06 | 2 minutes, 1080p; first global-access video-gen product at quality |
| Kling 1.5 | 2024-09 | higher resolution, better motion |
| Kling 1.6 | 2024-12 | image-to-video + motion control |
| **Kling 2.0** | **2025-04** | **3 minutes + multi-shot coherence** |
| Kling 2.1 Master | 2025-Q3 | further quality and duration improvements |

## Text-to-video competitive landscape (2024-25)

| Product | 2024 share of generated video clips | 2025 trajectory |
|---|---|---|
| Runway Gen-2/3 | ~20% | declining share |
| Pika | ~15% | declining |
| Sora | ~10% (delayed launch) | growing (post-Dec 2024 release) |
| **Kling** | **~35%** | **continuing lead on clip count** |
| Luma Dream Machine | ~10% | growing (US specialty) |
| Veo | ~5% | growing |
| Hailuo (MiniMax) | ~5% | growing (China) |

Kling's share by clip count substantially exceeds its share by "cinema-quality output" — i.e., many of its clips are casual-consumer rather than professional. This matches Kuaishou's broader consumer-video-platform positioning.
