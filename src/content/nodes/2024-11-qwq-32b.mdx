## What it is

A 32-billion-parameter reasoning model released November 28, 2024 as "QwQ-32B-Preview" by Alibaba's Qwen team. Built on the Qwen 2.5-32B base with RL post-training on reasoning traces (similar in spirit to OpenAI's o1 recipe, though Alibaba did not disclose detailed training specifics). At launch, the strongest open-weight model on math benchmarks (AIME, MATH-500) by a substantial margin.

QwQ-32B-Preview was the first open model to clearly exceed GPT-4o-class non-reasoning models on math, demonstrating that the reasoning RL recipe added meaningful capability that scaling alone hadn't.

## What QwQ contributed methodologically

While the formal training recipe was not published, the release showed:

- A 32B base model could host the reasoning capability — no need for GPT-4-class compute.
- RL on verifiable-reward problems (math, code) generalized somewhat to non-verifiable reasoning (the GPQA improvement, though smaller, was real).
- The "extended thinking" outputs were highly verbose (often 5,000-15,000 tokens for one response) — the inference-time cost trade-off was real.

These findings preview what DeepSeek R1's much more detailed training disclosure would document in January 2025.
