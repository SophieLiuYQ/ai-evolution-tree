## Llama 3 vs concurrent peers (Q2 2024)

| Model | Date | Org | Params | MMLU | HumanEval | GSM8K | License | Cost / 1M out |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| GPT-4 Turbo | 2023-11 | OpenAI | undisclosed | ~86% | ~85% | — | API | $30 |
| Claude 3 Opus | 2024-03 | Anthropic | undisclosed | 86.8% | 84.9% | 95.0% | API | $75 |
| Gemini 1.5 Pro | 2024-02 | Google | undisclosed | 83.7% | 71.9% | 91.7% | API | $21 |
| **Llama 3 70B** | **2024-04** | **Meta** | **70B** | **82.0%** | **81.7%** | **93.0%** | **open** | **~$0.90 (Together)** |
| Llama 3.1 405B | 2024-07 | Meta | 405B | 88.6% | 89.0% | 96.8% | open | ~$5 (Together) |
| Claude 3.5 Sonnet | 2024-06 | Anthropic | undisclosed | 88.7% | 92.0% | 96.4% | API | $15 |

The 405B variant approaches Claude 3.5 Sonnet quality at one-third the price (when self-hosted via Together / Fireworks). For high-volume workloads this is the operative comparison.

## What 15T tokens of training data looks like

Meta's data card disclosure:
- **>5% non-English** (vs &lt;2% for prior open models)
- **>4% code** in pretraining (Code Llama-style)
- ~50% deduplicated CommonCrawl
- Quality filtering using Llama 2 itself as a classifier

The data composition + scale is the technical moat. Meta does not disclose the specific quality filters, which are widely considered the highest-leverage piece.

## Llama 3.1 405B — the open frontier

Released July 23, 2024 with Meta's "Open Source AI Is the Path Forward" manifesto from Mark Zuckerberg. Specs:
- 405B params, 16k context (extended from 8k)
- Trained on H100 GPUs, ~16k of them, ~30M GPU-hours total
- First open-weight model whose training compute exceeded GPT-4's estimated training compute
- Cost to host: ~$5/1M output tokens at Together AI launch pricing

405B is the line in the sand: open weights at GPT-4-class scale, free to download, free to commercial-use (subject to the >700M MAU carve-out).
