## What it is

An encoder-only transformer pretrained with two objectives:
1. **Masked Language Modeling (MLM)** — randomly mask 15% of input tokens, predict them from bidirectional context.
2. **Next Sentence Prediction (NSP)** — given two sentences, predict whether B follows A. (Later work, RoBERTa, showed this objective was unnecessary.)

The bidirectional context was the key insight — every token sees every other token in the input, unlike GPT-1's left-to-right.

## How it compares to GPT-1

| Dimension | GPT-1 (2018-06) | BERT-base (2018-10) | BERT-large (2018-10) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Params | 117M | 110M | 340M |
| Direction | left-to-right | bidirectional | bidirectional |
| Objective | LM | MLM + NSP | MLM + NSP |
| GLUE avg | 72.8 | 78.3 | 80.5 |
| Released weights | no | yes | yes |
| Best for | generation | classification, QA, NER | same |

BERT was strictly better at understanding tasks; GPT was better at generation. The two design choices defined NLP architecture decisions for years.

## Why it shaped the field

BERT's open-weights release + Hugging Face's `transformers` library (launched ~the same time) created the modern "download a pretrained model and fine-tune in 50 lines" workflow. This was as important as the model itself for industry adoption.
