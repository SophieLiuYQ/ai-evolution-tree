## The agentic delta

Real-world agentic capability isn't fully captured by static benchmarks. Anecdotal but consistent reports from production users in mid-2025:

- Claude Code sessions complete 60-80% of well-scoped engineering tasks end-to-end with Opus 4, vs 30-50% with 3.7 Sonnet.
- Mean time-to-drift (when the agent loses the thread) extended from ~45 min to ~3 hours.
- Cost per completed task dropped despite Opus 4 being priced higher than Sonnet 3.7 — fewer retries, shorter total context.

These deltas are why Cursor, Windsurf, and Devin all defaulted to Opus 4 within weeks of release.

## Successor: Opus 4.6, Opus 4.7

Anthropic shipped Opus 4.5 (October 2025), 4.6 (December 2025), and 4.7 (early 2026) on a roughly 8-week cadence — each a substantial capability and reliability update without re-incrementing the major version. The 4.6 release added the 1M-token context window variant currently powering many long-document and large-codebase workflows (and likely the model writing this very file).

By early 2026 the Opus 4.x line is the de facto default for any agentic-AI workflow that requires sustained reasoning, tool use, and multi-hour autonomy.
