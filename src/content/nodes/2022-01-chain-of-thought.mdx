## What it is

A prompting technique: instead of asking "Q: [problem] A:", ask "Q: [problem] A: Let's think step by step." or provide few-shot examples that include reasoning chains. The model then emits its reasoning before the final answer, and the final answer is substantially more accurate on multi-step problems.

```
Standard prompt:
  Q: Roger has 5 tennis balls. He buys 2 more cans, each with 3 balls.
     How many balls does Roger have now?
  A: 11.    ← often wrong

Chain-of-thought prompt:
  Q: [same]
  A: Roger started with 5 balls. 2 cans × 3 balls = 6 new balls.
     5 + 6 = 11 balls. The answer is 11.    ← much higher accuracy
```

## Why this matters mechanically

The transformer's forward pass has bounded compute per token. Multi-step reasoning that doesn't fit in one forward pass simply can't be done in one shot. CoT works because it lets the model spread reasoning across many tokens, each of which gets its own forward pass.

Implication: CoT is a way of buying *more compute per query* by emitting more tokens. This insight — which the Wei et al. paper noted but didn't dwell on — became the foundation for o1 / R1 reasoning models two and a half years later.

## What CoT enables (and doesn't)

- **Helps**: math, code, multi-step planning, common-sense reasoning chains, tasks where intermediate steps are checkable.
- **Doesn't help (much)**: factual recall, simple translation, single-step classification, anything where the answer is "in the model" without computation.
- **Backfires**: high-stakes single-token answers where the chain introduces errors, or trivial tasks where the chain adds noise.

## CoT's three children

1. **Self-consistency** (Wang et al., 2022) — sample multiple CoT chains, take majority vote of final answers. Free accuracy gain at the cost of N× inference compute.
2. **Tree-of-Thought** (Yao et al., 2023) — explore CoT branches as a search tree with pruning.
3. **Reasoning models** (o1, 2024-09; R1, 2025-01) — train the model to do internal CoT automatically, optionally invisibly. The descendants where this technique becomes a product category.
