## What it is

A 34-billion-parameter open-weight foundation model with a 6B sibling, released on November 5, 2023. Architecturally a decoder-only transformer that closely mirrors Llama 2 (RoPE, RMSNorm, SwiGLU, GQA), trained on 3T tokens of bilingual (Chinese + English) data with a custom tokenizer optimized for Chinese.

Yi-34B's MMLU edged Llama 2 70B with half the parameters — the result that drove its Hugging Face leaderboard position. Within a month, Qwen-72B (released by Alibaba in late November 2023) overtook it on Chinese benchmarks; Mixtral overtook it on English benchmarks via MoE efficiency.

## The architecture-attribution controversy

Within 48 hours of Yi-34B's Hugging Face release, an open-source contributor (Eric Hartford) noted that Yi-34B's `config.json` was nearly identical to Llama 2's — including tensor naming conventions like `gate_proj` / `up_proj` / `down_proj`. 01.AI's response: the architecture was deliberately Llama-compatible (to leverage existing tooling), but the weights were trained from scratch on 01.AI's own data pipeline.

The community largely accepted this explanation but the episode established a norm: Chinese open-LLM releases now publish detailed pretraining methodology to preempt similar attribution debates (Qwen 1.5+, DeepSeek V2/V3, GLM-4 all include extensive pretraining data and recipe disclosure).
