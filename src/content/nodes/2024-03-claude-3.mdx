## What it is

A family of three multimodal models with shared architecture but different capacity / cost / latency trade-offs:

| Tier | Position | Launch pricing ($ per 1M tok, input/output) | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Opus** | flagship | $15 / $75 | complex reasoning, long documents, agentic work |
| **Sonnet** | balanced | $3 / $15 | most production tasks |
| **Haiku** | fast | $0.25 / $1.25 | high-volume classification, simple chat |

All three support 200k-token context and vision input (text output only).

Opus was the first model to exceed GPT-4 on most benchmarks by non-trivial margins. The HumanEval delta (+17.9) was particularly notable — Claude became the code-generation workhorse in many developer workflows and the default model in Cursor.

## The tiered-pricing innovation

Before Claude 3, LLM providers mostly shipped one flagship per generation with a secondary "turbo" or "instruct" variant. Claude 3's three named tiers — each a differentiated product — forced:

- **OpenAI** to clarify tiering (GPT-4o + GPT-4o-mini, May/July 2024)
- **Google** to relabel Gemini into Ultra / Pro / Nano (Feb 2024, pre-empted)
- **Mistral** to release Large / Medium / Small (Feb 2024, same week)

The economic effect: enterprises can now route traffic across tiers by task, dropping blended cost per query by 3-10× without quality loss. This is the operational foundation of "agentic" deployments (where a single user request spawns dozens of sub-model calls).
