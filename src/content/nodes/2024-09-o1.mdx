## Capability step-change in math and code

| Benchmark | GPT-4o | o1-preview | o1 | Human reference |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AIME 2024 | 13.4% | 56.7% | 83.3% | top 500 in US ~75% |
| Codeforces | 11th %ile | 62nd %ile | 89th %ile | — |
| GPQA Diamond | 53.6% | 73.3% | 78.0% | PhD expert ~70% |
| MATH | 76.6% | 85.5% | 94.8% | — |
| LiveCodeBench | 38.2% | 76.5% | 89.0% | — |
| MMLU | 88.7% | 90.8% | 92.3% | — |

The "uniform" gains on text-only benchmarks (MMLU) are modest. The differential gains on verifiable-reward benchmarks (math, code, science) are extreme. This pattern — reasoning models help where there's a checkable answer, much less where there isn't — has held across all subsequent reasoning-model releases.

## The hidden reasoning trace controversy

OpenAI showed users only a "summary" of the reasoning trace, not the trace itself. Stated rationale: prevent competitive scraping for distillation training. Researcher critique: the hidden trace makes safety audits, jailbreak research, and capability benchmarking structurally harder. As of mid-2025, no frontier reasoning lab has reverted to fully visible reasoning traces.
