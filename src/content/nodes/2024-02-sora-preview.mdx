## What it is

OpenAI's text-to-video generative model, first demonstrated February 15, 2024 with a set of research-preview videos. Architecturally a Diffusion Transformer (DiT) operating on spatiotemporal latent patches: the input video is encoded into latent space (similar to Stable Diffusion's VAE), divided into spacetime patches, and a transformer denoises the patch sequence conditioned on text.

The OpenAI write-up framed Sora as a "world simulator" — the suggestion that scaling video diffusion learned implicit physics (objects persisting through occlusion, fluid dynamics, lighting consistency) was a key narrative choice.

Sora's 60-second 1080p preview was the quality / duration ceiling-breaker. Subsequent product releases (Sora 1.0 Dec 2024, Sora 2 Sep 2025) gradually closed the gap between demo capability and shipping product capability.

## The "world simulator" framing

OpenAI's research write-up emphasized emergent capabilities — Sora apparently learned 3D consistency, object permanence, basic physics — and framed video diffusion as an early step toward a "general world model." This framing had implications for the broader narrative around scaling laws: maybe scaling video pretraining also produces useful world-knowledge representations, not just video output.

The framing was also commercially important: it positioned Sora not just as "OpenAI also has a video generator" but as "OpenAI has a foundational world-modeling capability that happens to ship today as video." The research-preview-then-9-months-of-iteration release pattern was unusual; most labs ship product upon demo.
