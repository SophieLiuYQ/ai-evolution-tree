## What it is

The fast/cheap tier of the Claude 4.x family, released October 1, 2025 (the date encoded in its model ID `claude-haiku-4-5-20251001`). Architecturally a smaller-parameter member of the Claude 4 family, but capable of extended-thinking mode — a tier feature that prior Claude generations restricted to Sonnet and Opus.

## Claude 4.x tier matrix (Q4 2025 → 2026)

| Tier | Best for | Reasoning? | Context | Price ($ / 1M out) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Opus 4.x | maximum capability, long agentic tasks | yes | 200k → 1M (4.6+) | $75 |
| Sonnet 4.x | production workhorse | yes | 200k | $15 |
| Haiku 4.5 | high-volume, background agents | yes | 200k | ~$1-2 |

The tier strategy mirrors what Anthropic established with Claude 3 (Mar 2024) but at higher absolute capability per tier. Haiku 4.5's MMLU (~85%) is comparable to Claude 3 Opus's 86.8% from 18 months earlier.

## Why fast-tier reasoning matters

Pre-2025 reasoning models were exclusively flagship-tier: o1-pro, Claude 3.7 Opus-only initial, Gemini 2.5 Pro. The implicit cost-per-query was $0.10-$5+, suitable for one-shot human queries but uneconomic for autonomous agents executing thousands of background tasks.

Haiku 4.5 made reasoning available at ~10× lower per-query cost. The downstream effect on agent product economics was substantial — autonomous coding, autonomous research, autonomous customer support all became viable use cases through 2025-26.
