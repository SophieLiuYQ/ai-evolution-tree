## What it got right vs wrong

| Claim | Status |
|---|---|
| Loss is a smooth power law in N, D, C | ✓ holds, replicated across labs |
| Compute-optimal split: scale model fast, data slow | ✗ Chinchilla showed the opposite |
| Architecture details are second-order | ✓ for transformer family |
| Predictable extrapolation works | ✓ within the regimes studied |

Even with the wrong scaling exponent, the *framing* — that capability-from-compute is a quantitative engineering problem — is the paper's load-bearing contribution.

## Practical impact

GPT-3 (May 2020), Gopher (Dec 2021), and PaLM (April 2022) were all sized using Kaplan-style fits. The under-training they implied (relative to Chinchilla's later corrected fits) is part of why Llama 2 (Jul 2023) — sized correctly — punched above its parameter count.
