## Training cost claim

Per DeepSeek's technical report, full training cost decomposed as:

| Stage | GPU-hours (H800) | Cost @ $2/hr |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-training | 2.664M | $5.328M |
| Context extension | 0.119M | $0.238M |
| Post-training | 0.005M | $0.010M |
| **Total** | **2.788M** | **$5.576M** |

This excludes "the costs associated with prior research and ablation experiments on architectures, algorithms, or data" — a substantial caveat. Independent estimates of the fully-loaded cost (including research compute, infrastructure amortization, salaries) ranged $50M-$1.6B. The reported number is the marginal compute cost of the final training run only.

V3 hit GPT-4o-class on MMLU, exceeded GPT-4o on math, was somewhat behind on coding. The pricing — $1.10 per million output tokens, with caching pushing it to $0.27 — was 14-55× below GPT-4o's API.

## What V3 led to

- **DeepSeek R1** (2025-01-20): RL post-training on V3 for reasoning capability; the version that triggered the global AI policy and market response.
- **DeepSeek V3.5 / V4** (2025): incremental refinements following the same playbook.
- **Industry follow-on**: every major open-weight lab (Qwen, Mistral, Llama 4) shifted toward MoE-default architectures in 2025; auxiliary-loss-free routing became standard.

V3 is the architectural ancestor of essentially all serious open-weight 2025 frontier work.
