## What it is

A reinforcement learning training pipeline that bypasses hand-designed reward functions:

1. Collect pairs of agent behaviors (trajectories).
2. Ask human annotators which trajectory is better.
3. Train a neural reward model to predict the human's preference.
4. Use the reward model as the reward signal for policy gradient RL.

The 2017 paper demonstrated this on Atari games and MuJoCo simulated robotics — the agent could learn to backflip, play Pong, etc., without any hand-coded reward.

## Why it sat dormant for 5 years

The 2017-2021 period saw the technique applied to a few research domains (summarization with RLHF, 2020) but did not break out. Two changes triggered the 2022 explosion:

1. **Capable base models**: GPT-3 was good enough that the RLHF policy had something worth aligning. RLHF on a weak model produces marginally less-bad weak output.
2. **Scaling annotation**: OpenAI invested in industrial-scale preference annotation pipelines (later: Scale AI, Surge). The 1.4M preference comparisons in InstructGPT would have been logistically infeasible in 2017.

The conceptual framework was Christiano's; the productization was InstructGPT's. Both papers belong in the lineage.

## Subsequent variants

- **DPO** (Direct Preference Optimization, 2023-05) — same data, no explicit reward model, simpler training. Now the dominant variant in open-source LLM post-training.
- **RLAIF / Constitutional AI** (2022-12) — replace human preferences with AI critique against written principles.
- **Process supervision** (PRM, "Let's Verify Step by Step", 2023-05) — reward each reasoning step, not just the final answer. Foundational to o1 / R1 reasoning models.
