## What it is

A neural-network system that takes an amino acid sequence as input and produces predicted 3D atomic coordinates for the folded protein. Architecturally:

1. **Multiple Sequence Alignment (MSA) input** — homologous sequences from related species, providing evolutionary signal about which residues co-vary
2. **Evoformer** — 48 transformer-style blocks that update both an MSA representation and a pair representation (between-residue features)
3. **Structure module** — produces atomic coordinates from the learned features, with iterative refinement
4. **End-to-end training** — backpropagation through the entire pipeline from sequence to structure

## CASP14 — the moment biology accepted AI

The Critical Assessment of Structure Prediction (CASP) is a biennial blind benchmark where computational methods predict structures of proteins whose true structures are known but unpublished. In 2020 (CASP14), AlphaFold 2 scored a median GDT (Global Distance Test) of 92.4 — comparable to experimental methods. The next-best entry scored ~62. The structural biology community publicly accepted the problem as "essentially solved."

## The AlphaFold Database

After publishing the model weights and code, DeepMind partnered with EMBL-EBI to compute structures for the entire UniProt database. By 2024, ~200M predicted structures were public — vs the ~200k experimentally-determined structures accumulated over 60 years of structural biology.

This database alone is now cited in tens of thousands of biology papers per year. The comparison to "before" is not "slower experimental methods" — most of these proteins simply had no known structure at all before AlphaFold. AI didn't speed up an existing process; it created a new dataset that didn't previously exist.

## AlphaFold 3 (2024)

Extends to predicting structures of proteins + DNA + RNA + ligands together — i.e., complexes relevant for drug binding. Released as Isomorphic Labs' AlphaFold Server (closed access) rather than open-source, which generated significant scientific community discussion about precedents for openness in AI-biology collaborations.
