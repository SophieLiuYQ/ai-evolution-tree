## How it compares to concurrent image generators

| Model | Date | Org | Approach | Weights | Quality (FID COCO) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| DALL·E 1 | 2021-01 | OpenAI | discrete VAE + autoregressive transformer | closed | — |
| GLIDE | 2021-12 | OpenAI | pixel-space diffusion | partial | 12.24 |
| DALL·E 2 | 2022-04 | OpenAI | CLIP + diffusion prior + upsampler | closed | 10.39 |
| Imagen | 2022-05 | Google | T5-XXL + cascaded pixel diffusion | closed | 7.27 |
| Midjourney v3 | 2022-07 | Midjourney | diffusion (details undisclosed) | closed | — |
| **Stable Diffusion 1.4** | **2022-08** | **Stability AI** | **latent diffusion + CLIP** | **open** | **12.63** |
| Stable Diffusion XL | 2023-07 | Stability AI | 2.6B params, better text encoder | open | ~8 |
| FLUX.1 | 2024-08 | Black Forest Labs | 12B rectified flow transformer | open (dev) | ~6 |

SD 1.4's quality was behind the closed competitors at launch — but the open weights + consumer-GPU tractability mattered more than the quality gap. By SDXL (2023-07) the gap had closed.

## What changed after release

- **Civitai** launched within 2 months — today hosts >100k community fine-tunes
- **Midjourney v4** quality leap (late 2022) was partly a response
- **ControlNet** (Feb 2023) added structured conditioning (pose, depth, edge maps) that closed models lacked
- **LoRA fine-tuning** popularized by SD — now standard across all generative AI
- **Getty Images v Stability AI** (2023) — still-unresolved copyright case that will shape generative AI law
