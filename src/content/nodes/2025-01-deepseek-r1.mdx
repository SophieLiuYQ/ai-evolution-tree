## R1 vs o1 — the comparison that moved markets

| Dimension | OpenAI o1 (2024-09) | DeepSeek R1 (2025-01) |
|---|---|---|
| Org | OpenAI (US, closed) | DeepSeek (China, open) |
| Architecture | undisclosed | 671B MoE / 37B active |
| Weights | not released | open (MIT license) |
| Training data | undisclosed | partial disclosure |
| Training compute (reported) | undisclosed (likely >100M GPU-hr) | 5.6M GPU-hr |
| AIME 2024 | 83.3% | 79.8% |
| MATH-500 | 96.4% | 97.3% |
| GPQA Diamond | 78.0% | 71.5% |
| API cost / 1M output | $60 | $2.19 |

The 27× cost difference at near-comparable benchmarks is the headline number that drove the market reaction.

## The Sputnik framing — and the pushback

Within 72 hours of release, R1 was the most-discussed AI release since GPT-4. The "Sputnik moment" framing dominated US tech press. Pushback emerged within weeks:

- The reported $5M training cost excluded research compute, ablations, and infrastructure — full-cost estimates landed in the $50M-$500M range.
- DeepSeek had likely distilled outputs from o1 / Claude / GPT-4 in pretraining (denied by DeepSeek; widely suspected based on sample analysis).
- The benchmark performance had gaps vs o1 on several axes (GPQA, real-world coding) that the headline "matches o1" didn't capture.
- The H800 GPUs used were a downgraded export-compliant variant; H100 access would have changed the picture.

By Q2 2025 the consensus had moderated: R1 was a real and significant achievement, but the framing as "China matched US frontier at 1% the cost" had been substantially walked back.

## Permanent effects

Regardless of the contested specifics, R1 made permanent the following:

- **Open-weight reasoning is a category** — Qwen QwQ, Sky-T1, Mistral Magistral, all followed within months.
- **US export-control debate intensified** — R1 became Exhibit A in arguments both for tighter chip export restrictions and against them (depending on whether you read it as "controls failed" or "controls slowed but didn't stop").
- **Closed-model pricing pressure** — OpenAI, Anthropic, Google all repriced reasoning tiers downward in February-April 2025.
