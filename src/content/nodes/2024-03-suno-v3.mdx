Suno's product velocity through 2024 outpaced research labs significantly. Whether this advantage holds depends on the copyright lawsuits and on whether Big Tech labs (OpenAI, Google, Meta) eventually ship competitive consumer products.

## The copyright case

In June 2024, RIAA-affiliated labels (Sony Music, Universal Music Group, Warner Music) filed lawsuits against Suno and Udio in US federal court alleging willful copyright infringement during training. Suno's response (filed August 2024) acknowledged training on copyrighted music but argued fair-use defense. The case is procedurally pending as of 2026 and is one of the most-watched AI copyright cases globally — the outcome affects training-data strategies across the entire generative AI industry.
