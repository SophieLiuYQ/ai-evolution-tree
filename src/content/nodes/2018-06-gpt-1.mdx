## How it compares

| Model | Year | Params | Approach | GLUE |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ELMo | 2018-02 | 94M | LSTM + bidirectional pretraining + feature extraction | ~71 |
| **GPT-1** | **2018-06** | **117M** | **Transformer + LM pretraining + fine-tune** | **72.8** |
| BERT-base | 2018-10 | 110M | Transformer + masked LM (bidirectional) | 78.3 |
| BERT-large | 2018-10 | 340M | Same, deeper | 80.5 |

GPT-1 was the proof of concept; BERT was the much louder follow-up four months later. OpenAI doubled down on the decoder-only direction — that bet paid off five years later.

## SWOT

- **Strengths** — First to operationalize transformer pretraining + fine-tuning. Established the GPT family architecture that didn't change for the next 7 years.
- **Weaknesses** — Smaller and less performant than concurrent BERT. Requires task-specific fine-tuning (no zero-shot).
- **Opportunities** — Set up GPT-2 (10× scale) and the eventual scaling-laws thesis.
- **Threats** — BERT's bidirectional masked LM dominated NLP benchmarks 2018-2020; GPT-1's relevance is mostly historical.
