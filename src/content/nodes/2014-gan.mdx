## What it is

A GAN trains two networks simultaneously:

- A **generator** $G(z)$ maps random noise $z$ to a sample that should resemble the training data.
- A **discriminator** $D(x)$ assigns a probability that $x$ is a real sample rather than a generated one.

They are optimized adversarially: $D$ tries to classify correctly, $G$ tries to fool $D$. At equilibrium, $G$'s distribution matches the data distribution and $D$ cannot do better than chance.

The value function:

$$
\min_G \max_D \; \mathbb{E}_{x \sim p_{\text{data}}}[\log D(x)] + \mathbb{E}_{z \sim p_z}[\log(1 - D(G(z)))]
$$

## How it works

```python
import torch
import torch.nn as nn

# simplified training loop
for real_batch in dataloader:
    # train discriminator
    z = torch.randn(batch_size, latent_dim)
    fake = G(z).detach()
    d_loss = -(torch.log(D(real_batch)).mean()
               + torch.log(1 - D(fake)).mean())
    d_loss.backward(); d_optim.step(); d_optim.zero_grad()

    # train generator
    z = torch.randn(batch_size, latent_dim)
    g_loss = -torch.log(D(G(z))).mean()
    g_loss.backward(); g_optim.step(); g_optim.zero_grad()
```

The adversarial objective is unstable in practice — one reason the first 3 years after the paper are a blur of architectural and objective-function variants (DCGAN, WGAN, LSGAN, StyleGAN).

## How to use it

The modern practical toolkit:

```python
# Using NVIDIA's pretrained StyleGAN3
import torch
G = torch.hub.load('NVlabs/stylegan3', 'stylegan3-t-ffhq-1024x1024')
z = torch.randn([1, G.z_dim])
img = G(z, None)  # 1024x1024 photorealistic face
```

For non-image domains (tabular data synthesis, time series), check `sdv` or `ctgan` packages.

## SWOT

- **Strengths** — Generates sharper samples than VAEs for a given compute budget. Unconditional sampling is fast (one forward pass). Latent space is often semantically meaningful (StyleGAN's interpolation properties).
- **Weaknesses** — Training instability is legendary — mode collapse, gradient vanishing, hyperparameter sensitivity. No explicit likelihood, so evaluation is hard.
- **Opportunities** — Real-time synthesis (gaming, video), domains where sample speed matters more than maximum quality, adversarial data augmentation for training classifiers.
- **Threats** — Diffusion models (2021+) surpassed GANs on photorealistic image generation. StyleGAN remains state of the art for faces specifically, but general-purpose image generation has shifted.

## Limitations

- **Mode collapse** — generator finds one or a few modes that fool the discriminator and stops exploring the rest of the distribution.
- **Non-convergence** — the minimax objective has no guarantee of finding the Nash equilibrium with gradient descent.
- **Evaluation is non-trivial** — FID and Inception Score are proxies; no clean likelihood.
- **Conditioning is tacked on, not intrinsic** — compare to diffusion, where classifier-free guidance is very clean.

## Why they remain relevant despite diffusion

- **Sampling speed** — a single forward pass vs 20-50 denoising steps. Critical for real-time applications.
- **Face domain** — StyleGAN's disentangled latent space is still unmatched for semantic editing.
- **Adversarial loss as a component** — used inside diffusion models and other generative systems as an auxiliary objective.
