## What it is

A three-step post-training pipeline applied to GPT-3:

1. **SFT** (Supervised Fine-Tuning) — train on human-written demonstrations of the desired output for a sample of API prompts.
2. **RM** (Reward Model training) — collect human rankings between pairs of model outputs; train a 6B reward model to predict which output a human prefers.
3. **PPO** (Proximal Policy Optimization) — RL fine-tune the SFT model to maximize the reward model's score, with a KL penalty against the SFT model to prevent reward hacking.

## The "1.3B beats 175B" headline

The most-cited result: human raters preferred outputs from the 1.3B InstructGPT model over the 175B GPT-3 base 85% of the time on the API prompt distribution. Two implications:

1. Alignment ≠ scale. Smaller, well-aligned models can dominate larger raw models on user-facing tasks.
2. RLHF is not free — it modestly hurts some benchmark scores while massively improving practical helpfulness. This trade was worth making.
